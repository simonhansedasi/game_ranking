Puzzle Scoring and Ranking
The plots below depict the distribution of scores for recent puzzles. The rectangle represents the 75th and 25th quartiles, and the black line shows the median of the distribution. The "whiskers" on top and bottom mark the end points of the distribution. A blue dot depicts the score submitted by the viewer.

The puzzle's difficulty rank D is calculated with the mean score μ, score standard deviation σ, and skewness of score distribution δ. μ would make for a decent ranking, however, the spread of the data is can also contribute to a difficulty ranking. Two puzzles can have a similar mean score, but a higher spread in the score data indicates a higher difficulty. σ is considered in this case to increase or decrease the difficulty ranking. The coefficient of variation, σ / μ is a unitless quantity that describes spread of a distribution to compare with other distributions. Finally, how the distributions are skewed, either towards high or low scores, is taken into account with δ. This helps score a distribution with outliers.

For Wordle, difficulty of the puzzle can be thought of as increasing with the score. The difficulty of Wordle puzzles is quantified as follows,

D = δ [ α μ + β σ μ ]

For Connections and Strands, lower scores indicate a harder puzzle, so the difficulty will increase inversely to the mean of the score distribution. A tiny decimal γ is added to avoid dividing by zero. The difficulty of these puzzles is quantified as,

D = δ [ α 1 μ + γ + β σ μ ]
